Projects

Dissertation: Effect of ply fiber orientation on the static and dynamic behavior of a composite swept-back wing | Sep 2024 - Jun 2025

  • Reduced the stress, deformation and increased frequency of a swept-back wing by tailoring the CFRP fiber orientation.
  • Role: Independent research project
  • Result: 15.92% stress, 9.29% deflection reduction at 30° fiber angle compared to full Al 7075-T6
  • Tools: SolidWorks, Ansys Fluent, Ansys APDL
  • Challenges: The project involves multiple simulation software tools, a strong proficiency in these tools is required to carry out the simulations accurately. Moreover, the transfer of aerodynamic loads between CFD and FEA was complicated by the use of different mesh discretisations. To resolve this, an optimal mesh independent study was initially established for the CFD analysis, which was then used as a reference to determine the optimal mesh for the FEA model.
